My Answers

This section provides the answers to the assignment questions
and the test results are further below

What do the test results mean to me?

I have done many of these tests over the last few years and I always find it hard to decide how to answer, should I select how I really feel inside versus how I act because I know it is expected of me. For example, even though I am not always the most talkative in a room, I can attend a dinner party full of strangers and drive conversation if I need to make an impression or if everyone is very reserved. I also feel unconfident most of the time but no one would ever describe me as such as I like to drive team outcomes and lead decision making. These results for me ended up just as I expected, they don’t entirely line up with how I think of myself but in some areas the comments were spot on. One item to note is that the results tend to be centred close to the middle rather than being very decisive one way of the other. I believe this reflects my point above but also shows that I can be adaptable in a team, tailoring my behaviour to suit the team dynamic.

The 16 personalities test was particularly accurate, reflecting that I naturally feel introverted, focus on the past, am self-conscious and sensitive to stress. All of these traits I understand and I make efforts to work around them so they aren’t a detriment to my work or family life.

My learning style test showed I like to learn by doing which is true but I think the visual learning style could actually be my strongest.

I found the big Five test least correct. I would have expected to score lower than 50% in Openness and much higher in Neuroticism.

How do you think these results might influence you in a team

I tend to be flexible in my behaviour in teams to fit in. I do have a tendency to be negative and overly positive personalities tend to grate on me after a time, so I have to be careful not to let that sort of reaction show. I like to take a place in a team where there is a gap to be filled, for example if there is a very strong leader I will follow rather than lead, or if there is someone more technically opinionated than me I will defer to their decision rather than make final technical decisions (as long as the end result will work of course). Because of this I tend not to get fixated on a particular role very often, I work hard and am passionate about the task at hand but am happy to change quickly to perform a different role if it suits the team. I do like autonomy however and find decision by committee rather wasteful, so if my opinion is not being followed I don’t push it, aligning with the next best idea to keep the momentum going.

How should you take this into account when forming a team?

The main results from these tests that I need to take into account when forming a team are ensuring there is a leader I respect and there is a balance between realistic negativity and positive personalities. Apart from that, I need to make sure there is someone with skills (or a willingness to learn) for every type of task, including a team leader. I don’t mind work taking longer if it means one team member is upskilling another resource, it builds depth in the team and I personally like sharing what I have learnt with others.

Because I can fill various roles, either technical, leadership, requirements capture, scoping or project management, it means that forming a team is sometimes easier as I can be flexible in the part I play. In addition, I can perform one or more roles temporarily to fill a gap due to resource overload or unexpected absences.
