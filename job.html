The Job - Service Owner Telecommunications| Energy Resources Sector

Job Description, skills required and my skills plan

This operational role is particularly interesting to me as it involves many different skills, both commercial and technical. There is a new outsourced delivery model to be implemented along with the responsibility for ongoing vendor management. The yearly operational budget is sizeable and there appears to be involvement in small projects such as improvement programs. Governance forms part of the role, including stakeholder management, technical standards, risk and continuous improvement. Technical experience comes into play directly with involvement in incident and problem resolution, change management and handover to operations from projects. This role would be ideal for me as the mix of responsibilities would always keep me interested. The operational nature of the role would allow me to build an annual routine for consistency, but with the everchanging technical and commercial landscape of telecommunications there will always be new technology to investigate and many opportunities to optimise costs and vendor services.

The skills, qualifications and experience required for this position include influencing skills, personal relationship building and management abilities, a university degree in IT, ITIL Foundations certification, networking technical certification (i.e. Cisco), experience in large scale vendor management, Oil and gas or Mining field experience, evidence of safety standards and procedure compliance and experience managing technical performance of telecommunications networks.

I possess most of the skills and experience required for this role, in fact my current role is somewhat similar, just not of the same scale and across different technology. I have managed various hardware and software systems in both insourced and outsourced environments. While my total operational budgets have exceeded $5m annually, individual vendor spend has always been less than $1m. I have been contract owner for one vendor contract but it has been simple and low cost, without strict SLAs to manage performance against. One large item missing for me however is technical skills, qualifications and experience with telecommunication networks. While I have always managed IT systems that rely on networks, I am not technically proficient in their design, configuration or operation.

My plan to obtain the skills and experience I require for this type of role is quite clear. The biggest task will be completing a degree and becoming technically proficient in telecommunications networks. In parallel to completing my degree, I could apply to move into the networking team to increase my telecommunications experience. In regards to commercial skills, I am not that far off where I would need to be and if I keep gaining work experience in this area while obtaining my technical qualifications. In terms of timing, my qualifications, technical and commercial skills and work experience should all line up nicely in around 5-7 years if I stick to this plan.
